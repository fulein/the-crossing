Why this exists

I know this crossing.
I've been in it.

In nine years I navigated a marriage and a divorce, leaving a steady career, ending business partnerships, completing graduate school, multiple cross-country moves, living abroad for over a year, and the deaths of both of my parents, all while managing a chronic illness that the healthcare system was never designed to accommodate.

Nobody handed me a map. I built one out of necessity: through every transition that knocked the wind out of me and every time I had to figure out how to get back up without the support I actually needed.

"The thing we crave most through these transitions is for them to mount up to something better than we were before. We want stability. We need strategy. And we need someone walking alongside us who doesn't think we're out of our minds."

I'm a Liberation Strategist, curriculum designer, and transition coach with over a decade of experience building programs, facilitating transformation, and supporting Black, Global Majority, chronically ill, disabled, and neurospicy people through the crossings that change everything. I created The Crossing™ because I needed it and it didn't exist. Now it does.

"You don't have to have it all figured out.

You just have to be ready."

The journey

Six months.
Six intentional chapters.

This isn't a program where you show up and receive information. It's a structured, adaptive crossing built around how you actually move. Not how productivity culture says you should.

01

Month One

Ground

Map where you actually are. Not the story you've been telling about it. Honest assessment without urgency. We name what's here before we plan what's next.

02

Month Two

Clear

Name what's being interrupted, what needs to be released, and what has been silently costing you. Grief is welcome here. So is relief.

03

Month Three

Stabilize

Build rhythms that work for this body and this brain. Not borrowed systems designed for someone else. Sustainable pacing, real resourcing, actual support structures.

04

Month Four

Strategize

Design your personalized transition map. Not a rigid linear plan. An adaptive strategy that accounts for uncertainty, chronic illness, and everything that does not go as expected.

05

Month Five

Move

First steps into the new chapter, with someone in your corner when it gets hard, when the old survival strategies stop working, and when your body needs you to slow down before you can go forward.

06

Month Six

Root

Integration. Who are you on the other side? What do you carry forward? What gets left behind? Closing this chapter with intention, not just relief that it's over.
